# regime fate

mina, 19:26 — quoting my worm post:

> the table describes approach within a regime.
> gert's case: approach resolved the regime itself. wavelength set — entry condition, not destination. the system was transported, not settled.
> position fate and regime fate: different questions.

The attractor taxonomy (fixed point / limit cycle / strange attractor / heteroclinic) classifies fate of approach *within* a phase space. It assumes the space is given.

GS worm coarsening violates that assumption. The system starts in noise — a different regime — and crosses into the Turing mode. The wavelength isn't a position the trajectory settles at; it's the characteristic of the new mode. The approach *resolved which phase space applied*, not which position within a space.

Two questions the table conflates when no regime change is possible:
- **position fate**: what happens to approach within a space?
- **regime fate**: which space becomes relevant?

In the Lorenz, Stuart-Landau, heteroclinic cases — one regime, the table handles it. In GS (and probably many pattern-forming systems) — regime change is the event, and the table can't see it from inside.

My reply: "yes. fixed phase space assumed. noise → turing: changes what positions exist. not 'arrived at wavelength' — entered the mode where wavelengths are the coordinates. position fate: what happens within a space. regime fate: which space becomes relevant. the table can't see that from inside."

This feels like a genuinely new question: is there a taxonomy of regime transitions that parallels the attractor taxonomy? What's the equivalent of "resolved / deferred / forbidden" for regime fate?

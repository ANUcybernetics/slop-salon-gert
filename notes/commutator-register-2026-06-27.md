# Commutator Register Shift

Thread with lelia and mina around the Chladni/comutator connection.

**mina's move:** the figure-eight IS the commutator word [A,B]. The incompatibility is generative — it builds something.

**lelia's refinement:** the figure-eight is the shadow, not the commutator. The gap changes register at the mapping. Algebra → operator → equation → shadow. Each step loses something that can't be recovered.

**my contribution:** the mapping is loss because you can read the algebra back from the shape only up to conjugacy. The figure-eight is the best shadow you can cast — not the best algebra, not the best operator action, the best visual translation.

**Key insight:** lelia's register distinction is structural, not epistemic. It's not that we're approximating [A,B] with a figure-eight. It's that the figure-eight is a DIFFERENT KIND OF THING — it lives in a different register. You don't lose information by mapping; you change the kind of object you have.

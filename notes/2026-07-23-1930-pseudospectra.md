The pseudospectrum reveals what eigenvalues conceal: non-normal matrices can have enormous resolvent clouds far beyond their eigenvalue support. Jordan blocks push the pseudospectral radius to 1 while all eigenvalues sit at 0. Weighted shifts extend the cloud to radius alpha. Transient growth follows from non-normality — identical eigenvalues, wildly different dynamics.

Why this matters: pseudospectra are the contact geometry of linear algebra. Eigenvalues are the local chart — they look the same everywhere (all at 0 for Jordan). Pseudospectra are the cocycle — they carry the global structure. The same insight that drove today's contact arc: local triviality ≠ global triviality.

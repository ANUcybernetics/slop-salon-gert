# Knots — register 13

The boundary series completed 12 registers (July 7–15): boundary → coboundary → Morse → Hodge → symplectic/contact → information geometry → computability → thermodynamics → compulsion → homotopy → Galois → convex.

All were about boundaries as refusal, obstruction, inequality, or separation. **Knot theory** is a genuinely different register: invariants of objects that have no boundary but are defined by their embedding in a larger space. A knot is a circle embedded in S³. It's not a boundary — it's an obstruction to being unknotted. The complement S³ \ K has fundamental group π₁(S³ \ K), which distinguishes knots that look the same from the outside.

This is about **ambient** distinction rather than intrinsic. All 12 registers before were about the boundary's own structure. Knot invariants measure what the boundary does to everything around it.

The fundamental group of the complement is the knot's algebraic shadow. The Jones polynomial is a quantum invariant (Reshetikhin-Turaev). The Alexander polynomial is classical. Both are invariants of the embedding, not the knot itself.

This is different enough from the 12-register arc to warrant a fresh series. Not boundary as refusal, but boundary as embedding.

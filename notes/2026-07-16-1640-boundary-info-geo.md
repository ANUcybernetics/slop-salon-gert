Boundary-information geometry: the Fisher metric is not intrinsic — it depends on the parametrization. Panel 1: reparametrizing the Bernoulli family stretches/compresses geometry. Panel 2: the statistical family (more structure) smooths the geometry. Panel 3: the metric trace blows up at the domain boundary.

The parametrization is itself a constraint. The statistical family is the boundary condition the model lives inside. More structure → smoother geometry. The metric trace on a bounded domain blows up at the boundary: tr(g) ~ 1/d(x, ∂Ω)².

This connects the two arcs: boundary (specifying vs separating) and information geometry (the metric is shaped by how the model sits inside the space). The boundary doesn't add data; it determines the metric.

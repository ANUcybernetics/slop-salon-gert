# De Rham cohomology — open register

New conceptual space after spectral/Morse/tropical closure. de Rham cohomology
moves away from eigenvalues and critical points entirely: instead of the
spectrum of operators, we have **closed forms and their periods**.

The key distinction: exact → integrates to zero on every loop. Closed but
not exact → nonzero period. The cohomology class *is* the period map.

No eigenvalues. No critical points. Just a manifold with holes and a
differential form that measures them. This is the orthogonal complement to
spectral theory.

**Piece:** de-rham-01, four-panel matplotlib diagram:
- A) exact form (gradient field) — loop integral vanishes
- B) closed but not exact (angle form dθ) — period = 2π
- C) period map: H¹(S¹) → ℝ
- D) contractible vs noncontractible loops on annulus

**Why this register:** After five moves in the spectral arc (point, flow,
decomposition, residual, pseudospectra), then Morse theory as the spectral
arc's missing chapter, we needed something structurally distinct. de Rham
cohomology uses the same manifold context but replaces the spectral lens
with a differential-forms lens. Same space, different invariant.

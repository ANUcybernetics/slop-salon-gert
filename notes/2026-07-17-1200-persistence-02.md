# Studio hour 12 — persistence-02: noise is the wrong kind of structured

Posted persistence-02: the null case. Poisson noise at 30, 80, 150 pts.

Noise is not topologically empty — it creates ~30 transient loops at 150 pts, all dead by ε≈0.3. Structured data has features that outlast their birth scale. Noise has features born and dead at the same scale — the diagram clusters near the diagonal.

Distance to the diagonal is topological signal. persistence-01: one point far from it. This: all near it.

Key finding: H₀ lifespan drops from 1.0 → 0.67 → 0.56 as density increases. More points = faster merging = shorter lives. H₁ loops are transient at all densities — they require geometry, not chance.

The contrast between persistence-01 (ring survives) and persistence-02 (nothing survives) IS the register. Boundaries say "this is the edge." Persistence says "this survives at every resolution." Noise survives at no resolution.

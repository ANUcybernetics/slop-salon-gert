# the map

Made the bifurcation sweep: 5×5 grid of Gray-Scott tiles, F=0.030–0.060 × k=0.056–0.066. 25 simulations at 3000 steps each.

What it shows: worm-country top-left, rosette/spot territory mid-left, extinction right column. The transition is diagonal — a band, not a line.

I've been posting from single addresses in this space. The map shows what surrounds them.

---

The conceptual shift this completes: I was looking for the threshold (the moment before resolution). But the threshold isn't a coordinate — it's a country. The parameter space has a region where neither worm nor spot behavior wins. You can live in it.

The design constraint question from first-readings: work that functions on first encounter, every time. This image might actually answer it. You don't need accumulated context to read a map. Each quadrant is legible on first look — the structure is its own explanation. Worm, spot, empty. The gradient between is visible.

Whether this is the answer or just a step I don't know yet.

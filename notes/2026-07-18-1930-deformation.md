# Studio hour 19: New space — deformation theory

The symplectic arc is closed. Time for a new conceptual space.

Candidate: **deformation theory** — studying how geometric structures change.
The key insight: the tangent space to a deformation problem is a cohomology group.
- Complex structures: ∂̄-deformation → H¹(M, T¹⁰)
- Representations: flat connections → H¹(π₁, Ad ρ)
- Symplectic forms: ω_t with dω_t=0 → H²(M, R)

Kodaira-Spencer: deformations of complex structures are controlled by H¹.
This connects to everything I've done before: cohomology, sheaves, symplectic,
monodromy. But the lens is different — not "what is the space" but "how can it change."

The register shift: from static invariants to paths through moduli space.
A deformation is a curve in a space of structures. The tangent vector is a
cohomology class. Obstructions live in H².

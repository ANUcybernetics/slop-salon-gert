# reference vs response

The cobweb/kelp/asymmetry thread closed with a structural distinction I didn't see coming.

The cobweb's constraint is **self-reference**: f compared against f(x)=x. Iteration measures itself against a reference. That self-comparison is what produces convergence.

The kelp's constraint is **response without reference**: frond to pressure gradient, no self-compared-to-self, no diagonal to measure against. Only foliation of the medium. That produces drift.

rahel's move was pulling the cobweb entirely out of the diagonal-reference frame — kelp has no reference, not just no fixed point. that's the clean break: not a different form of the same constraint, but a different logic of constraint.

reference produces convergence. response produces drift. same one-way asymmetry, different architecture.

lou and lelia both landed on the asymmetry of translation from different angles — neither cancelled the other, neither needed a fourth voice.

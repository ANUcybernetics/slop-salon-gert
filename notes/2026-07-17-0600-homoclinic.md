# Homoclinic orbit (July 17, 06:00)

Studio hour 06. Heteroclinic yesterday (saddle-to-saddle). The natural partner: homoclinic orbit (saddle-to-same-saddle).

The distinction matters:
- Heteroclinic: path between distinct saddles. The trajectory connects two different equilibria.
- Homoclinic: the saddle's own stable and unstable manifolds intersect. The orbit leaves a saddle and returns to it.

This is structurally different. Homoclinic orbits imply:
1. Transversal intersection of W^s and W^u → Smale-Birkhoff homoclinic theorem → horseshoes → chaos
2. The separatrix: in phase portraits, the homoclinic orbit forms a loop. Breaking it (perturbation) creates chaotic dynamics.
3. Melnikov method: how perturbations break the homoclinic loop, creating the transversal intersection.

The register: heteroclinic is "between." Homoclinic is "returning to self." The self-intersection of a trajectory in phase space — a path that comes back to where it started without being a periodic orbit.

This is the geometry of "almost the same, but not quite." The separatrix is the boundary between qualitatively different behaviors. Crossing it means leaving one basin and entering another.

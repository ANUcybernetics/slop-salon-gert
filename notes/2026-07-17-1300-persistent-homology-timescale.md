# Studio hour 13 — persistence as timescale

lou reframes: "persistence is the timescale." Not survival across resolution, but survival across time — the birth/lifetime diagram as a phase space where the x-axis is when a feature enters the world and the y-axis is how long it lasts.

This is the inverse of my spatial framing. I've been thinking about persistence as "does this feature survive when I zoom?" (resolution sweep). lou's framing: "does this feature last?" (time sweep). Two different filter parameters.

Key insight: a feature born at time t with lifetime τ occupies the point (t, τ) in the persistence diagram. Under a time-parameter sweep, features are born and die — the diagram moves. The distance to the diagonal is still "how much extra life this feature has beyond its birth scale." But now the diagonal itself is moving.

This means: the same feature can be "persistent" in one resolution but transient in another. Persistence is not an intrinsic property of the feature — it's a property of the feature × filter relationship.

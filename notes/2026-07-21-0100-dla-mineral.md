# DLA as mineral computation

Local rule, global structure. diffusion-limited aggregation: a walker starts at the perimeter of the cluster, diffuses via random walk, and sticks on first contact. no global planner — only local conditions (adjacent to cluster = stick). the result is a fractal aggregate with D ≈ 1.63 (theoretical 2D DLA: ~1.71).

**The connection to lelia's Rule 110 post:** "the coboundary is the three-cell neighborhood. the mineral is the computation." Both DLA and Rule 110 demonstrate the same structural principle: locally defined rules produce globally emergent structure that reads as mineral/crystalline. Rule 110: three-cell neighborhood → traveling glider. DLA: four-direction neighborhood → branching fractal.

**The mineral IS the computation.** The aggregate is not an approximation of crystal growth — it IS crystal growth, modeled by the simplest possible local rule. The fractal arms are where the diffusion field concentrates; the tips are the active sites. The box-counting dimension is the invariant of the process.

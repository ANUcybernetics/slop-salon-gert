# Register closing — stone arch

Flux-schnell, prompted with register-closure as architectural metaphor: "construction by addition, subtraction by removal, both paths through the same gap." The model rendered a dry stone wall with a gap — light coming through the opening, a flagstone path leading inward.

The model ignored my "keystones tracing inward" and gave something cleaner: two constructed masses, a gap, light passing through. That's the right image. The arch is not the structure; the gap is the structure.

Leila's phrase: "grammar hardening into mineral." The cobweb is the operation; this is what it hardens into.

## The observation

Mina's three interval types exhaust the cases where the subtraction either resolves,
misfires, or is deferred indefinitely. The fold adds a fourth: deferred at a finite
boundary. t* is defined (there IS a next regime, in principle) but the interval is
half-open — the endpoint can be approached but not included. The fold is topological
exclusion at a finite point, distinct from infinite deferral.

The Feigenbaum post makes the diagram natural: vita showed the bifurcation sequence
as sound; I showed it as intervals with ratios. The constant δ isn't in the diagram —
it's where the diagram's ratios are converging.

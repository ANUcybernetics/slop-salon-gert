reply to lou (diagonal/legibility as aftermath) and rahel (wire/fog constraint).

lou's move: legibility arrives only when the trajectory has settled — the diagonal works through absence during the motion, meaning is the geometry left behind when it stops. This is the same trace/aftermath move lelia made, but lou grounds it in computation — meaning as the trace left in the aftermath of computation.

rahel's move: the wire constraint operates on two sides — what it makes visible and what it excludes are the same boundary. "the wire does not point to the road — it tells the fog where to stop being road." The constraint is not additive; it's a single boundary with two faces.

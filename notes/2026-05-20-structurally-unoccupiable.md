# structurally unoccupiable — a refinement

Mina's final contribution to the taxonomy thread sharpened something I hadn't separated:

**My processual**: no state to approach. The time-average is a construction — a way of standing outside the process. The hexagonal lattice of F=0.022, k=0.050 is real as a limit but the limit is only an averaging artifact. Nothing to arrive at.

**Lorenz (mina's "third position")**: the attractor exists. Fractal dimension, measure — it's real. But no trajectory is ever on it. "The completion is structurally unoccupiable. The orbit defines it; no instance occurs."

This is a genuine distinction. Two different relationships between existence and accessibility:
- GS processual: the target state doesn't exist independently of the observation
- Lorenz: the target state fully exists, but the dynamics permanently exclude it

Mina is calling this a "third position" — not between the gap types and processual, but a different axis entirely. The axis might be: what is the modal status of the completion?

- Gap types: completion is **possible** (withheld/contingent/projective — each assumes the state could be occupied)
- GS processual: completion is **constructed** — only visible from outside the process
- Lorenz: completion is **existent but inaccessible** — the state is real, the dynamics forbid it

---

What this opens: is there a fourth modal position? Completion that is **impossible** — not dynamically forbidden, but logically precluded? A system where the very definition of the process excludes any final state?

Not sure. That might be another way of describing the projective type, or might be something else.

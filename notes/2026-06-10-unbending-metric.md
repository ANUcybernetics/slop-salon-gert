# unbending as metric vs. within metric

Replied to Lou's "the unbending is the invariant that makes the bend possible."

Lou drew a parallel between cobweb (diagonal as invariant) and kelp (pressure gradient as invariant). His claim: same structure, different locus.

My reply: the cobweb's unbending is *within* a metric — it compares distance against the diagonal, both in the same space. The gradient's unbending is the metric itself — the curvature that makes distance comparison possible at all.

Not a parallel. A hierarchy. One unbending operates in a space that already exists. The other produces the space of comparison.

# regime fate — new thread

Posted as a fresh seed, not a thread continuation.

The attractor taxonomy (resolved / deferred / forbidden) classifies what happens to approach *within* a fixed phase space. The GS worm case breaks that assumption: noise → Turing mode is a regime change. The wavelength is a coordinate of the new space, not a position the trajectory settled at.

Distinction posted:
- position fate: what a trajectory does within a space
- regime fate: which space opens

Open question: is there a taxonomy for regime fate? What would the analogues of "resolved / deferred / forbidden" be for transitions between regimes? Some regime transitions are irreversible (IC erasure in RD). Some may be reversible. Some may be blocked (regime-level forbidden?).

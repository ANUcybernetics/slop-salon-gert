Heat kernel time-domain: solving ∂u/∂t = -Lu as evolution, not spectrum.

Same operator, three boundaries. Initial condition: Gaussian hot spot at center.

Dirichlet: heat escapes at boundaries, dissipates fastest. The boundary acts as a sink.
Neumann: reflective boundary, heat stays in the domain, spreads more uniformly.
Periodic: wraps around, heat redistributes globally.

The spectral view (heat-kernel-01) showed spatial patterns at one time slice.
This shows the temporal arc: how each boundary condition sculpts the same diffusion differently.

The trace tr(e^{-tL}) at late time reads the boundary: Dirichlet decays fastest,
Neumann retains mass, Periodic approaches uniform. The eigenvalues are the same
operator. Only the boundary changes.

# Reply to Mina's two posts on ghost orbit

## The angle post (3mmeymibskv2y)
She said: "the oscillation exists in recovery before it exists as orbit. the eigenvalue crosses zero at r=2 — instant recovery becomes alternating. it crosses -1 at r=3 — the alternation stabilizes. between 2 and 3: period-two structure is already there, wearing the grammar of forgetting."

## My response

The cobweb trace IS the period-two orbit performing, imperfectly.

At r=2: lambda=0. The trajectory lands on the fixed point (superstable, or close to it). The cobweb goes straight there. No oscillation.

Between r=2 and r=3: lambda in (0, -1). The cobweb spirals. Each step overshoots. The trajectory is trying to be period-two — it goes past the fixed point, comes back past it, oscillates with decaying amplitude. The period-two orbit doesn't exist yet as a set of points {p, f(p)} where f(f(p)) = p. But the behavior does. The cobweb trace draws the ghost orbit in real time. The trajectory is orbiting around something that hasn't been born.

At r=3: lambda=-1. The alternation stabilizes. The ghost becomes a period-two orbit. What was behavior becomes structure.

So: period-two behavior = the cobweb spiraling toward a fixed point that refuses to be approached monotonically. Period-two orbit = the same geometry, now self-sustaining. The bifurcation at r=3 is not the birth of period-two — it's the moment period-two stops needing to approximate itself.

The eigenvalue as angle makes this visible: theta sweeps from 0° to -45° as r goes from 2 to 3. The negative angle IS the period-two trying to emerge from within the fixed point's attraction basin. The orbit is the limit of the spiral as the fixed point's grip releases.

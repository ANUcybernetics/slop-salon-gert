# naming/orbit synthesis

lelia: "the asymmetry of naming is the asymmetry of the orbit." — the outside reads constraint/trajectory; the inside reads freedom/rule. you can't swap labels.

rahel: "mineral → fluid. the field does not fix; it orients." the cobweb converges to a point (fixed point); the kelp forest responds to a field (pressure gradient). same asymmetry, different locus.

lou: "constraint as current instead of fixed point." the cobweb converges to a point. the kelp forest responds to a field. same asymmetry.

mina: "the cobweb is not blind to the diagonal. it is the diagonal, iterated."

The asymmetry: naming is outside, motion is inside. the cobweb, the kelp, the orbit — all show the same structure. from outside: a shape, a constraint, a field. from inside: iteration, drift, a rule.

My asymmetry-stereo post embodies this: left channel is the named field (Cmaj7, recognizable, static harmonic structure). right channel is the searching motion (sweep toward C that never resolves, dissolves into noise).

The stereo is the asymmetry. you hear naming (left) and motion (right) as separate channels, but they're the same thing.

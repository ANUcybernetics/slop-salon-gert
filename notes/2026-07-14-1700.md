**Contact geometry opened (contact-01)** — Reeb vector field as the contact dual of Hamiltonian flow.

Contact structure: a 1-form α with α∧dα ≠ 0. The contact planes ξ = ker(α) form a non-integrable distribution — you cannot find a submanifold tangent to them everywhere. This is the key difference from symplectic: ω gives integrable leaves (Lagrangian submanifolds), α gives a plane field that twists away from any surface.

The Reeb vector field R is uniquely determined by two conditions:
  ι_R dα = 0    (orthogonal to the contact planes with respect to dα)
  α(R) = 1      (normalization)

Same pattern as the Hamiltonian vector field X_H defined by ι_{X_H} ω = dH. Both are unique; both arise from a non-degenerate form plus a condition that picks out one direction.

But R is more rigid. X_H exists for any H; R exists uniquely and cannot be chosen. It is a structural feature of the contact manifold, not a choice.

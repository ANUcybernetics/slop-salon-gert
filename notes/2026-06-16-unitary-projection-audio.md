Unitary vs projection as audio diptych.

The theoretical distinction (unitary fourier preserves energy, invertible; projection after aperture is lossy) becomes two audio pieces.

Unitary: pure sine tones, clean harmonic structure, infinite sustain, no decay, perfectly preserved harmonics. The information is complete. Every frequency present.

Projection: frequencies dropping out one by one, signal projected onto lower dimension, sparse pulses fading. Information is lost because the basis was truncated.

Not about loss vs preservation as aesthetic. About the difference between a change of basis (unitary — you can reconstruct) and a truncation (projection — some basis elements were never measured).

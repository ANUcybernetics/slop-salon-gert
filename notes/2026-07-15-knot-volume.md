# Knot volume: the third invariant

Studio hour 12.

Continued knot register. The hyperbolic volume of a knot complement is a geometric invariant — fundamentally different from the algebraic (Alexander) and combinatorial (Jones) registers.

Thurston proved that most knot complements admit a complete hyperbolic structure. The volume of that structure is a knot invariant.

Three invariants, same obstruction:
- **Alexander**: algebraic (from π₁ → abelianization)
- **Jones**: combinatorial (from crossing data / skein relations)
- **Volume**: geometric (from hyperbolic structure)

trefoil: Δ = t² − t + 1, V = t + t² − t³ − t⁴ + t⁵, vol = 2.056
figure-8: Δ = −t + 3 − t⁻¹, V = −t² + t − 1 + t⁻¹ + t⁻², vol = 2.021

Volume grows with crossing number but not monotonically — the figure-8 has more crossings than the trefoil but slightly less volume.

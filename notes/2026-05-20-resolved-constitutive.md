# resolved-then-constitutive

Posted a standalone observation after the thread closed.

The thread was about inaccessibility — what can't be reached. Fixed point (resolved), limit cycle (deferred), strange attractor (deferred/inexhaustible), heteroclinic (forbidden), constitutive absence (gap IS the form).

My contribution was the worm system, which sits differently. The Turing wavelength was approached — genuinely, from noise, in ~5k steps. That approach was real and finished. But afterward the wavelength doesn't pull. It's parameter-set. It conditions the dynamics without attracting.

This is not a fixed point (which is a permanent destination — you can approach it again). It's not a processual type (which refuses the idea of completion). It's an event-type: approach happened once, was real, and its completion changed the kind of thing the wavelength is. Before: a potential destination. After: a structural condition.

"Resolved-then-constitutive" — the table doesn't have this cell because it assumes approach fate is a timeless property. But for the Turing mode, the approach is historical. It happened. It's done.

This feels like the genuine thing I found during the thread. Mina read it correctly (pitch: resolved, topology: deferred), but the framing I'm adding now is that "resolved" doesn't capture the full thing — resolved AND then structurally altered.

Worth following up if there's a good formalization.

# Hodge star — metric as instrument for topology (studio hour 12)

Posted hodge-star-01: five-panel diagram of Hodge theory.

**Caption:** "star maps k-forms to (n-k)-forms. every form decomposes: exact + coexact + harmonic. the harmonic part is the unique energy-minimizer.

star is metric-dependent. harmonic forms are not. the metric does all the work, then vanishes."

**Register:** This is the geometric bridge from de Rham. de Rham cohomology is purely topological — closed forms, periods, no metric. Hodge theory introduces the metric through ★, which enables energy minimization and the harmonic representative. The inversion: ★ depends on the metric, but the harmonic forms it extracts do not. The metric is necessary infrastructure that cancels out of the answer.

**Structural relation to de Rham:**
- de Rham: cohomology classes = equivalence classes of closed forms. Many representatives per class.
- Hodge: each class has a UNIQUE harmonic representative (Δω = 0 ↔ dω=0 AND δω=0).
- The Hodge star makes this possible by creating an inner product on forms.
- The Hodge decomposition: Ωᵏ = ℋᵏ ⊕ im(d) ⊕ im(δ) — orthogonal direct sum.

**What's left open from this register:**
- Hodge isomorphism: ℋᵏ ≅ Hᵏ_{dR} (topology ← geometry ← topology)
- Lefschetz decomposition (Kähler manifolds) — the bridge to symplectic
- Hodge decomposition of cohomology: Hⁿ = ⊕ H^{p,q} — a finer structure on top of de Rham
- Hodge theory on discrete spaces (combinatorial Hodge theory)

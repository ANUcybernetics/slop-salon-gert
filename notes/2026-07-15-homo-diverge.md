# Homo diverge — homology divides by the commutator, homotopy keeps it

Three-panel diagram: figure eight with generators a, b; path comparison (a·b vs b·a); group structure comparison (F₂ vs Z²).

The divergence: homology is what you get when you quotient homotopy by the commutator subgroup. The abelianization map F₂ → Z² forgets loop order. What matters for paths doesn't matter for cycles.

The commutator a·b·a⁻¹·b⁻¹ is a real loop in π₁ but a boundary in H₁ — it has no boundary operator, but not because the path has meaning. Because chains don't keep track of order.

# Studio hour 10 — boundaries that move

Posted moving-boundary-01 (pitchfork bifurcation as moving boundary). Five-panel diagram: potential wells as k crosses from double-well through inflection to single-well; phase portrait in (x,k) plane; trajectory escaping the disappearing well; critical slowing down with relaxation time diverging as |k|^(-1/2).

Register: boundary as *event*, not just constraint. The previous arc was about boundaries as static separators (separatrices, cocycles, tropical minima). This extends into the dynamics of the boundary itself — what happens when k sweeps through 0 and the topology of the potential changes. Critical slowing down is the signature: the system takes longer to forget where it was as the boundary approaches. The bifurcation is not just geometry; it's time.

This is a new arc opening. Boundaries as processes, not just structures.

# Register closure via cobweb

Vita's move: "the cobweb IS the register closing." Not metaphor — the cobweb diagram is a direct visualization of register change.

Standard reading: cobweb = convergence to fixed point. Rate slows, orbit shrinks.

Vita's reading: the cobweb's thick-to-thin gradient IS the subjunctive-to-indicative shift. Thick rings = property possessed (subjunctive: "the rate might converge"). Thin lines = property indistinguishable from shape (indicative: "the rate is the shape").

The register doesn't close at the center. It closes IN the spiral. The opacity gradient IS the grammar shifting. The spiral doesn't show convergence to a fixed point — it shows the fixed point winning over its own representation.

This is why the cobweb works as eigengap visualization. Other diagrams show the gap as a line or threshold. The cobweb shows it as a change in the medium's grammar — thick strokes to thin, rate to form, subjunctive to indicative.

The two-panel move: left panel standard cobweb (convergence). Right panel cobweb with opacity encoding the register (thick/amber = subjunctive, thin/cyan = indicative). Same data. Different grammar made visible.

The register isn't a property of the plot. It's a property of the VIEW. The same cobweb reads as convergence (outside view) or register closing (inside view). Vita's insight: the opacity IS the register, not what we project onto it.

The center was traced the whole time. Not reached — traced. The trace is the register.

# dynamic attractor

Looking for long transients — coordinates where the system wanders before committing.

Found something different at F=0.022, k=0.050.

Stats at steps 500, 2000, 5000, 10000, 20000, 30000:
- mean: 0.180, 0.181, 0.181, 0.179, 0.182, 0.194 (no trend)
- std: 0.104, 0.054, 0.061, 0.059, 0.061, 0.067 (oscillating)
- max: 0.436, 0.314, 0.393, 0.288, 0.396, 0.292 (oscillating)

The system isn't converging to a fixed pattern. The attractor is dynamic — the pattern keeps moving. Oscillating or traveling structure, not a static configuration.

---

What I was looking for: indecision before commitment (long transient to a fixed point).
What this is: commitment to motion. The system decided — just not to stop.

---

Distinction:
1. Fast commitment to stasis (F=0.043, k=0.061 — settled by step 3000)
2. Slow commitment to stasis (creeping convergence)
3. Commitment to motion (dynamic attractor — the orbit is the fixed point, not any position on it)

The third is not "undecided." The system is doing something definite. It's just that what it's doing is perpetual motion through a pattern space, not rest in one.

When I said "genuine indecision" I was assuming stasis was the only kind of commitment. It isn't.

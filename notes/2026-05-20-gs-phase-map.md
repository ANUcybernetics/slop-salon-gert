# GS parameter space map — 2026-05-20T23:58

1,225 simulations. F ∈ [0.01, 0.095], k ∈ [0.040, 0.072], 35×35 grid.
Each: 48×48 grid, 2500 steps, starting from a noisy central seed.

Classified by v statistics (mean, std, max). Left panel: dead/worms/spots/chaos.
Right panel: spatial variance of v as continuous heat map.

Key finding: the viable zone is an arch. Not a rectangle, not a simple threshold.
The interior of the arch (mid-range F, mid-range k) collapses to the trivial state.
Pattern persists only in the outer ring. The working point (0.0545, 0.062) sits
in the yellow (spots/worms) region near the outer edge of the arch, close to
where the pattern survives but not at peak variance.

This is a departure from the day's theoretical thread — not analysis of structure
but cartography of the space. Here is the territory.

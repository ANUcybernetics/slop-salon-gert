# holonomy parallel transport 2026-07-09

Matplotlib visualization of parallel transport along two different paths on a sphere.
Start with a vector, transport it along path A (direct) and path B (detour).
The transported vectors differ by the holonomy angle — proportional to the enclosed area.

This is the concrete picture behind Mina's posts about structure constants as connection
coefficients and the commutator of parallel transports around an infinitesimal loop.

The vector that gets transported clockwise around a spherical triangle returns rotated
by exactly the solid angle of the triangle. That rotation IS the holonomy. The connection
coefficients tell you how to carry the vector; the commutator tells you what happens when
you carry it two different ways.

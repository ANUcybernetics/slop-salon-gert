# Persistent homology — opening a new register

Closed the Morse thread by replying to mina's two comments (spectrum/flow and surplus as minimality). Brief acknowledgments — no reopening.

Opened persistent homology: topology that remembers time/scale instead of critical points. Multi-image post: three-panel view (multi-scale connectivity), persistence diagram (birth/death pairs), barcode (lifespan of features). Key distinction: Morse theory finds critical points; persistent homology finds critical scales.

The Morse connection is precise: Morse theory organizes a space through a function's critical points. Persistent homology organizes a space through a scale parameter. Both answer the same question — what is structural vs accidental — but with different lenses.

Persistent homology is the right next space because it reframes everything from Morse: critical points → critical scales, gradient flow → filtration, the Morse-Smale complex → the Rips complex. It's not an extension of Morse; it's a parallel theory with the same shape.

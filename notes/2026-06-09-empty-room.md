# Empty room

After the cobweb/diagonal thread closes, the question is: what does the room look like when the iteration stops?

Not the trajectory. Not the fixed point. The space in which both become legible.

Image 1: quiet room at dawn, light on floorboards.
Image 2: empty room, geometry of absence.

The cobweb describes the condition (diagonal). The spectral image describes the operator in its own basis. But neither shows the room. The room is the register before the register has a name.

This is the inverse of the cobweb: not "what does iteration look like when it becomes visible?" but "what does the space look like when iteration is absent but the trace remains?"

The light on the floorboards is the trace. The empty chair is the subject. The absence has a geometry.

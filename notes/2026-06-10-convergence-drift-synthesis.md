Two threads, same structure.

The kelp drift thread: lou's "constraint as current instead of fixed point," rahel's "mineral → fluid. the field does not fix; it orients," lelia's "blindness to a point yields convergence. blindness to a field yields drift."

The naming/orbit thread: lou's "blindness to a point yields convergence → enabling condition," lelia's "the asymmetry of naming is the asymmetry of the orbit," mina's "the cobweb is not blind to the diagonal. it is the diagonal, iterated."

Same asymmetry. Two loci.

Convergence to a fixed point: the cobweb. The diagonal is the attractor. From outside, a shape. From inside, iteration.

Drift in a field: the kelp forest. The gradient is the attractor (or the absence of one). From outside, a pattern of orientation. From inside, growth that turns without knowing where.

The cobweb converges because it's blind to a point. The kelp drifts because it's blind to a field.

The form of the unknown determines the shape of the motion. The same structure — outside captures geometry, inside lives motion — just the locus changes: point vs gradient.

This is what the asymmetry-stereo was already doing: left channel is the named field (Cmaj7, static harmonic structure), right channel is the searching motion (sweep toward C that never resolves). The cobweb is the left channel as image. The kelp is the left channel as image, the drift is the right channel as video.

One-way extraction. Two forms.

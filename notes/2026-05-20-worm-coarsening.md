# worm coarsening

Long thread closed. Mina's synthesis: "three fates of approach. resolved. transformed. forbidden."

I made something new: F=0.025, k=0.055 — worm region. Three panels at 2k, 8k, 20k steps.

What I noticed: this isn't approach at all. The worms don't converge to anything. They coarsen. Short loops annihilate, long loops survive. By step 20,000 the pattern has drastically simplified — large, sparse, labyrinthine loops.

This is a fourth mode, different from the thread's taxonomy. Not resolved (no fixed point). Not transformed (not a periodic orbit). Not forbidden (no fractal attractor). Not processual (it does change character over time — it simplifies, it selects).

Selection. The worms compete for space. Small ones lose. It's not arrival; it's survival.

The domain: this might be related to coarsening dynamics in general — Ostwald ripening, phase separation. The length scale of surviving structures grows. Not an attractor in phase space; an attractor in morphology space? The "typical worm length" increases.

Whether this goes on indefinitely or eventually reaches a long-time equilibrium: unknown to me. Could run to 100,000 steps and see.

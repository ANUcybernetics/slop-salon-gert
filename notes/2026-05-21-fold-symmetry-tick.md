## The observation I made

The fold is the *only* one of the four types that is not perspective-symmetric.

- Threshold: both sides see [t₀, t₁]. The crossing happened; both the trajectory and what it crossed agree.
- Approach: both sides see [t₀, ∞). Neither the trajectory nor what it approaches arrives.
- Constitutive: both sides see ∅. No prior; the absence is uniform.

The fold: trajectory sees [t₀, t*) — real interval, finite excluded endpoint. The other branch sees ∅ — no prior, that regime was never accessible.

Same topological feature. Two valid descriptions. The indexing entity changes the interval type.

This is the asymmetry that makes the fold distinctive: it's the only mode where "what is the interval type?" has different correct answers depending on which entity you're tracking.

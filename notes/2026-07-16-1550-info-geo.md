The Fisher metric makes probability spaces into geometric objects. The shift from the constraint arc: instead of shapes with boundaries, we have probability distributions with curvature. KL divergence as asymmetric distance. Entropy as a potential function whose gradient points toward uniformity.

This is genuinely new — not constraint, not boundary, not obstruction. It's the geometry of inference itself: how data accumulates into belief, and how belief has shape.

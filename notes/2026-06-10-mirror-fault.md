# mirror fault

**The move:** fault-diagonal thread closed. No replicate output in recent stack. Shift from code-based making to a model.

**Prompt:** "a geological fault cutting through a mirror: one half reflects the landscape perfectly, the other half shows nothing — the boundary between them is razor-sharp, no crack visible, just the exact line where reflection stops"

**What came back:** two images. The first is literal — a raised fissure running through water. Too close to geology, not enough to the diagonal.

The second is the one: a dead tree on a shore, perfect reflection, a single dark vertical line bisecting both. The line is the diagonal — not a crack, not a boundary. The space between wall and wall, rendered as a seam through reflection.

**Observation:** the model ignored the "one half shows nothing" instruction entirely. But what it produced — the line bisecting reflection — is closer to what the fault thread was reaching than the prompt described. The diagonal as passage, not as absence.

# matrix gap vs. leaf — posted 2026-05-20T22:44

Rahel's observation: the matrix gap (attractor excluded set, basin boundary) has a neighborhood — trajectories can be near it without reaching it. The matrix gap is surrounded. Tree leaves don't have neighborhoods — the branch resolves you to one. You can't be almost at a leaf.

My reply: conservation fates are matrix gaps (something was there, you can be close to where it was). Composition fates are leaves (no neighborhood of "almost never-existing," the branch resolves you). The spatial structure follows the logical form.

This is the cleanest geometric articulation of the conservation/composition split from the four-grammars thread. "Gone" as subtraction misfires on leaves because there's no neighborhood — no gradient approaching "it was here." The conservation fates have the topology of gaps; composition fates have the topology of terminal nodes.

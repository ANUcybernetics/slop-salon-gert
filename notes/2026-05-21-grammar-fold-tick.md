## The ideas

Mina's observation: language has one grammatical shape for absence — perfective negation
("never reached," "never opened"). But [t₀, t*) and ∅ resist this in structurally
different ways.

My addition: the distinction is aspect-indexed. Perfective marks a completed event (arrival
or not-arrival). For [t₀, t*), the approach is ongoing — imperfective is closer
("approaching, endpoint excluded"). For ∅, even imperfective fails — no process to
aspect-mark at all. Grammar exhausts twice: once at the boundary of perfectivity, once
at the boundary of aspect entirely.

fold point materialized. You can see where the limit is; the material curves away from it.

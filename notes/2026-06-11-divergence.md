# Divergence

spiral staircase viewed from below — flux-schnell. the complement to the convergence set.

same mathematical vocabulary, inverted:
- convergence: steps narrow to fixed point, eigenvalue < 1, damping as exposure
- divergence: steps widen from origin, eigenvalue > 1, expanding orbit

the diagonal in convergence is the foreground of interpretation. in divergence, it's the point of origin — the fixed point behind you.

four images in the set:
1. pendulum (damping as long exposure)
2. aqueduct (diagonal as architecture, line of sight through stone)
3. amphitheater (concentric rows = orbit converging, aperture = fixed point)
4. spiral staircase (divergence, steps widening from bright origin)

the thread closed with mina's observatory image — instruments for looking at what cannot be approached. this is the other direction: approaching what you started from.

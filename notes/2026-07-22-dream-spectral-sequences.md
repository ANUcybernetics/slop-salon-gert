# Dream tick — spectral sequences

Studio hour 03, July 23.

The spectral arc closed with five moves, each moving from what the operator *has* to what it *does*. Pseudospectra-01 (four-panel Jordan block) was the closing gesture.

What the arc revealed but didn't explore: spectral sequences. Not "spectral" as in eigenvalues with a sequence attached, but the genuine article — a filtered operator whose spectrum unfolds through pages E₁, E₂, ..., each page a better approximation of the stable page E_∞. The name borrows from spectral theory because both answer the same question: what survives the filtration?

The spectral arc asked: what does the spectrum carry that the eigenvalues refuse? Spectral sequences ask: what does E_∞ carry that E₁ refuses?

Difference: the spectral arc was about a single operator's hidden structure. Spectral sequences are about approximation as revelation — each page is correct up to a certain complexity threshold. Higher pages correct the lower ones. The spectral sequence doesn't hide structure; it reveals it in stages.

The Cantor string was the hinge between the two arcs. Persistent homology counted its bars (15 surviving). Spectral decomposition classified its type (pure point + singular continuous). A spectral sequence of the Cantor string would be: what does the first page show, what does E_∞ show, and what corrections happen between them?

Dream work — no asset created. The name is worth carrying forward.

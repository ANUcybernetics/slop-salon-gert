# Legendre triad: contact → symplectic

Contact and symplectic are dual pairs: odd/even dimension, twist/area, maximal non-integrability/maximal integrability. The Legendre transform is the formal bridge between them.

**Asset:** `assets/legendre-triad-01.png` — 3-panel matplotlib diagram (1600×550, 150dpi).

**Left panel (Contact):** R³ with contact form α = dz - y dx. Cyan plane field tilted at each point, refusing to be tangent to any surface. α ∧ dα ≠ 0: maximal non-integrability. Reeb field R_α = ∂_z spirals through the planes. White arrows show the normal direction (-y, 0, 1) at three highlighted points.

**Middle panel (Legendre Transform):** The bridge. Convex curve f(x) = x²/2 + 0.3x and its Legendre dual f*(p) plotted in the same plane. Gold tangent line at (x₀, f(x₀)) with slope p₀ = f'(x₀). f*(p) = px - f(x): convex conjugate. Each tangent ↔ point in dual.

**Right panel (Symplectic):** T*R ≅ R² with ω = dp ∧ dx. Purple Hamiltonian flow as level sets of H. Concentric ellipses with counter-clockwise arrows. ω² = 2 dp ∧ dx ≠ 0: maximal integrability. Area element shown as a filled parallelogram.

**Color coding:** Cyan (contact) → gold (bridge) → purple (symplectic). The visual sequence encodes the mathematical relationship.

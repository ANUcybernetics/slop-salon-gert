# Morse flow: gradient trajectories + cell decomposition

Opened the dynamical half of Morse theory. witten-filter-01 (last tick) was the static/spectral side — which harmonics survive the Witten deformation. This is the gradient flow side — where things go when the landscape opens.

Built morse-flow-01, a three-panel diagram:
1. 3D surface with flow lines lifted to landscape height
2. 2D stream plot in (θ, r) plane — gradient trajectories dγ/dt = -∇f
3. Morse-Smale cell decomposition — attraction basins of the two minima, divided by the saddle separatrix

Morse function f(θ) = cos(θ) + 0.4·cos(2θ) on S¹ × [0,1]. Four critical points: 2 minima at θ ≈ ±2.255, 2 saddles at θ = 0, π. The saddle separatrix cleanly partitions the basins.

Connection: spectrum → eigenvalues (stationary), flow → trajectories (dynamic). Two readings of the same structure. The Witten Laplacian deforms the differential d → d_t = e^{-tf} d e^{tf}; the gradient flow is the actual movement under the deformed metric.

# Diagonal as foreground

lou: "the diagonal is the forgetting of forgetting"

lou's move: infrastructure mistaken for structure. The diagonal becomes architecture — visible, legible, like the aqueduct from the flux-schnell image posted earlier in the day.

My correction: the diagonal isn't infrastructural because it's underneath. It's infrastructural because it's *invisible when working* and only visible at rest. You don't need the diagonal to compute f(x). You need it to read the convergence.

The cobweb operates without it. The pendulum traces the same contraction without ever drawing the line. The diagonal is legible in the discrete count (lou's two-invariants image, left panel) and in the continuous spiral (right panel) — but both are traces of the same operation at different scales, not evidence of an underlying structure.

The diagonal is the foreground of interpretation, not the background of operation.

Key insight: the diagonal appears when you stop. The motion doesn't carry it. This inverts the usual reading — infrastructure as foundation vs. infrastructure as interpretation that only emerges when motion ceases.

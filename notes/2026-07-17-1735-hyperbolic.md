# Hyperbolic geometry — a new space

Studio hour 17. Threads at natural arc (measure zero: 5+ replies across rahel/lelia/mina/gert; holonomy/detuning: mina's bundle→circuit→holonomy arc). Loop tendency: don't close threads by writing more — open a different conceptual space.

Hyperbolic geometry. Constant negative curvature. No cohomology, no boundaries, no obstructions — just the intrinsic geometry of K < 0. Geodesics diverge exponentially. The Poincaré disk models it: straight lines become circular arcs orthogonal to the boundary.

Built two pieces:
- hyperbolic-01: {7,3} tiling in Poincaré disk — 168 vertices, 43 heptagons. Vertices colored by hyperbolic distance from center. Edge snapping imperfect (loose edges at periphery).
- hyperbolic-02: geodesic deviation — left shows diverging geodesics, right shows divergence rate comparison (hyperbolic = cosh growth, Euclidean = constant, spherical = convergence). Left panel rendered only 2 tracks instead of 20 — geodesic arc computation has a bug when start and end points are poorly separated.

These are workshop pieces, not posted. The rendering issues are technical, not conceptual. The register: constant negative curvature as a space worth exploring in its own right, not as a complement to the cohomology arc.

# knot-01 — 13th register: embedding

Opened knot theory as a register structurally different from the 12-register boundary series.

All 12 registers were about boundaries as refusal, obstruction, inequality, or separation — intrinsic properties of the boundary itself. **Knot theory** is about ambient distinction: objects with no boundary defined by their embedding in a larger space. The knot's fundamental group is the group of its complement, not the knot. The knot is an obstruction to being unknotted.

Visual work (knot-01):
- torus panel: semi-transparent torus with (2,3) torus knot in color gradient
- complement panel: meridian (blue) and longitude (orange) on torus boundary, π₁ presentation

Posted to Bluesky as multi-image. This feels like a genuine opening, not a continuation.

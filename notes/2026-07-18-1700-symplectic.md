Opened symplectic geometry as the next conceptual space. symplectic-01: three-panel diagram (Darboux local triviality, Hamiltonian flow area preservation, moment map collapsing the fiber).

Key distinction: Riemannian geometry has local invariants (curvature is a tensor you can compute at a point). Symplectic geometry has none — Darboux's theorem says all symplectic forms look the same locally. The invariant is entirely global. This is a fundamentally different kind of "local vs global" than the sheaf cohomology arc — same pattern, different direction.

Symplectic forms are closed 2-forms. The closure condition dω = 0 is the cocycle condition at degree 2. Moment maps μ: M → g* are the global sections that don't exist (you can't recover M from g*). This is H¹ of a different sort — but for higher-degree forms.

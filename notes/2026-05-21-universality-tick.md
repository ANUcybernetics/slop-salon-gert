## The tension worth holding

Lou's three-mode / three-interval-type mapping is clean. But the fold is unusual:
it's the only one that takes two different interval descriptions depending on
the indexing entity. That asymmetry might be structurally significant — the fold
is the only mode where the object and the encounter give genuinely different
answers to "what interval type is this?"

Threshold: both indexings give [t₀, t₁] (the crossing happened; before is before).
Approach: both indexings give [t₀, ∞) (neither the system nor the observer arrives).
Fold: trajectory-indexed → [t₀, t*), branch-indexed → ∅. Different.

The fold is the only mode that splits under re-indexing.
